About Dallas

Situated in the North Texas, Dallas is the main core of the largest metro area in the Southern U.S. It is the county seat of Dallas County with its portions extending into neighboring Denton, Collin, Kaufman, and Rockwall counties. Dallas constitutes relatively larger urbanized area as the Dalla-Fort-Worth (DFW) metroplex where one-quarter of all Texans live. The city has a total area of 385.8 sq. miles of which 340.5 sq. miles is land and 45.3 sq. miles is water.

A cultural hub!

Dallas is the region’s commercial as well as cultural hub. Downtown, at Dealey Plaza, you have the Sixth Floor Museum that commemorates the site of President John F. Kennedy’s assassination in 1963. The other places worth visiting here include the Crow Collection of Asian Art and the Dallas Museum of Art, in the Arts District. If you are a connoisseur of contemporary sculpture, the sleek Nasher Sculpture Center is a must visit for you.

Population and Ethnicity of Dallas

Dallas is the 4th largest metro area in the country with its combined statistical area being the 7th largest in the U.S. The city has a population from a diverse religious and ethnic backgrounds. The city has an enthralling history of thousands of years of varying cultures. It was inhabited by the Caddo people area before Spanish colonists claimed Texas in the 18th century as a part of New Spain’s Viceroyalty.
